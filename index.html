BikeShare Trends over a Day

With Seasonal Analysis!.

As shown above, there are seems to be three peaks for bikeshare ridership during the day: 8AM, 12PM, and 5PM. These times make sense since these times are either when most people are off from work or school or on a lunch break. Summer has the highest ridership at 12PM and 5PM. It should be noted that there were not enough datapoints for Spring, thus it would not be a fair comparison between the other seasons. Winter has the lowest ridership throughout the day if not including Spring.

  • Date: November 2018

Seaonal Duration Analysis

From our data analysis and visualization, bikesharing for all the 4 seasons seems to follow the general trend of Monday to Wednesday ridership duration being low, then it ramps up to peaking on Saturday, and finally it drops on Sunday. It becomes apparent that people ride the longest on Spring Saturdays in particular. This makes sense as people generally have the most free times on Friday, Saturday, and Sunday, which explains why they have the longest rider duration. Spring is generally the season in which people try to go out to smell the blooming flowers and feel sunshine which would have been missed during the winter. This would amplify some ridership duration, thus explaining for Spring Saturday having the longest rider duration. Some unexpected findings were that Spring Monday and Tuesday had the shortest ridership duration, but this could be a result of lack of data since spring had 1/10th the data of every other season

  • Date: November 2019
